Section One

Executive Summary

We were engaged by the Board of Directors of Al Rawabi Group to perform an independent assessment of the SAP enterprise system landscape across Emirates National Food Company (ENF) and Greenfields for Feed Industries (GF). This assessment was conducted to evaluate system readiness for a proposed S/4HANA transformation and to identify material gaps in current operational processes.

Assessment Opinion

In our opinion, the current SAP ECC implementation at ENF and GF entities does not adequately support the poultry industry operational requirements. Material deficiencies exist in livestock management, feed planning, production costing, and quality traceability that cannot be addressed through configuration alone. We recommend a Greenfield S/4HANA implementation with SAP industry-specific solutions.

Scope and Methodology

2.1 Engagement Scope

Our assessment covered the SAP ECC systems and related business processes at two entities within the Al Rawabi Group poultry division:

Entities in Scope
Entity Operations SAP Modules Active

2.2 Assessment Methodology

We conducted our assessment in accordance with NXSYS Advisory Standards for IT System Assessments. Our methodology comprised the following phases, with detailed procedures documented in Appendix A:

Phase 1: Document Review

We obtained and reviewed existing SAP configuration documentation, business process flows, and prior assessment reports. We noted a significant absence of formal documentation for custom developments and integration specifications.1

Phase 2: Workshop Sessions

We conducted cross-functional discovery workshops over a four-day period with key stakeholders from both entities. Sessions covered all major functional areas as detailed in Section 4.

Phase 3: System Walkthrough

We performed system walkthroughs to validate information gathered during workshops and to observe actual transaction processing.

Phase 4: Analysis and Reporting

Findings were analyzed, risk-rated, and subjected to our confidence scoring methodology as described in Appendix B.

Limitation of Scope

Our assessment was limited to the ENF and GF entities. Al Rawabi Dairy Company (ARDC) was not included in this engagement phase and will be subject to a separate assessment. Conclusions herein should not be extrapolated to ARDC operations.

Section Three

Key Findings

The following findings represent material deficiencies identified during our assessment. Each finding is rated according to our risk classification framework and assigned a confidence score based on the quality of evidence obtained. For the complete confidence scoring methodology, refer to Appendix B.

FINDING F-001
Absence of SAP Livestock Management Module
Critical
Condition

The current SAP ECC implementation does not include SAP Livestock Management functionality. Flock tracking, biological asset valuation (IAS 41), FCR monitoring, and vaccination management are performed manually using Excel spreadsheets and journal vouchers.

Criteria

Industry best practice for poultry operations requires integrated livestock management with real-time flock performance monitoring. IAS 41 requires biological assets to be measured at fair value less costs to sell, with changes recognized in profit or loss.

Cause

The original SAP ECC implementation did not include industry-specific solutions. The Livestock Management module was not available or not considered during initial deployment.

Effect

Management lacks real-time visibility into flock performance. FCR calculations are delayed and potentially inaccurate. Biological asset valuations are estimates rather than system-calculated. Audit trail for IAS 41 compliance is incomplete.

Confidence Assessment
90%

Based on: System configuration review, stakeholder interviews (5), process walkthroughs (3), documentary evidence review. See Appendix A, Procedure P-001.

Cross-References

FINDING F-002
Feed Management Without Store Control
Critical
Condition

Feed is delivered directly from suppliers to farm silos in bulk tankers (32.5 tons capacity) without passing through a central store. There is no measurement at silo level, no ability to transfer between farms, and excess feed is disposed as waste. Annual feed expenditure is approximately AED 55-60 million.

Criteria

Feed represents 95-98% of poultry production cost. Industry practice requires controlled feed procurement through stores, consumption tracking, and FCR-based planning to optimize the largest variable cost component.

Cause

Historical process design bypassed store function for operational convenience. Lack of silo-level measurement infrastructure. Absence of central planning function.

Effect

Current FCR of 1.7-1.9 versus industry benchmark of 1.4 indicates significant feed wastage. Unable to correlate feed supplier quality with flock performance. Potential cost leakage estimated at several million AED annually (as noted in Section 4.2).

Confidence Assessment
95%

Based on: Site visit observations, procurement records review, stakeholder interviews (7), FCR calculation verification. See Appendix A, Procedure P-002.

Section Six

Recommendations

Based on our findings, we recommend the following actions. Each recommendation is cross-referenced to the related finding and assigned a priority based on risk and implementation dependencies.

RECOMMENDATION R-001
Implement S/4HANA with Industry Solutions
Priority 1
Recommendation

Proceed with a Greenfield S/4HANA implementation incorporating SAP Livestock Management and SAP Meat & Fish Management (MSG) industry solutions. The current ECC system cannot be cost-effectively enhanced to address identified gaps.

Expected Benefits
  • Real-time flock tracking and biological asset valuation
  • Integrated feed planning with FCR optimization
  • Production costing with carcass splitting valuation
  • End-to-end quality traceability
  • IAS 41 compliance automation
Related Findings
F-001 F-002 F-003
Immediate Actions (Pre-Implementation)

While planning the S/4HANA implementation, we recommend the following immediate process improvements:

  • Establish central feed planning function with store intermediary
  • Implement co-packer contract management with confirmed order policy
  • Complete DOC/egg supplier specification rollout
  • Consider bag-based feed procurement for improved control
Section Seven

Opinion

Independent Assessor's Opinion

In our opinion, based on the assessment procedures performed and evidence obtained, the current SAP ECC implementation at Emirates National Food Company and Greenfields for Feed Industries does not adequately support the operational requirements of a modern poultry business.


We identified five critical findings relating to the absence of industry-specific functionality, inadequate feed management controls, deficient production costing, uncontrolled co-packer operations, and incomplete quality management. These deficiencies result in material operational inefficiencies and potential financial leakage estimated in the millions of AED annually.


We recommend that management proceed with a Greenfield S/4HANA implementation incorporating SAP Livestock Management and Meat & Fish Management solutions as the most viable path to address identified gaps and achieve target operational maturity.
