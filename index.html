Five Critical Gaps

Issues requiring immediate executive action

1

Wrong SAP Module

Using PP (discrete manufacturing) instead of PP-PI (process industries). Fundamentally wrong architecture for dairy and poultry processing.

Cannot capture process parameters, batch yields, or component tracking
2

QM Module Not Active

SAP Quality Management module not live. All quality processes paper-based. No LIMS at ARDC. Products released without system holds.

Major food safety and regulatory compliance risk
3

Integration Gaps

Weighbridge, LIMS, equipment, van sales all disconnected. 22 days of production without system receipts documented.

Inventory accuracy and audit trail compromised
4

Van Sales Invisible

300+ routes not created as SAP locations. No route-level P&L. All operations in external Ransale system with batch sync only.

Cannot identify profitable vs. unprofitable routes
5

Costing Inaccurate

Livestock not IAS 41 compliant. No component-level costing for dairy. FCR 25-30% above benchmark (1.7 vs 1.4).

Financial statements and product profitability distorted

Food Safety & Compliance

Critical gaps that expose the group to regulatory action and recall risk

Products Released Before Test Results

Microbiological results (5 days for yeast/mold) come after product is already in market. Management acknowledged: "We sleep with that risk."

Consumer safety risk | Potential recall liability | Brand damage

4-Hour Recall Capability Not Possible

Cannot trace finished goods back to raw milk supplier or flock within 4 hours. Batch-to-batch linkage breaks at production order level.

Regulatory non-compliance | Expanded recall scope | Audit failure

Process Parameters Not Captured

Critical parameters (pasteurization 72C/15sec, fermentation temp/time, cooking temps) NOT captured in SAP. Only exist on paper.

Cannot prove pasteurization | HACCP documentation gap

Vaccination & Medication Not Tracked

Flock health treatments recorded on paper. Cannot demonstrate compliance with withdrawal periods before slaughter.

Antibiotic residue risk | Export certification issues

Action Required

Recommendations Roadmap

Immediate (0-3 months)
Activate SAP QM Module

Enable inspection lots, usage decisions, quality notifications. Implement system-enforced holds pending micro results.

Integrate Weighbridge

Connect weighbridge to SAP for automatic GR posting. Eliminate the 22-day receipt gap.

Establish Batch Traceability

Implement parent-child batch relationships. Enable 4-hour recall capability.

Short-term (3-6 months)
Implement PP-PI for Process Industries

Migrate from discrete PP to process orders. Capture process parameters, component tracking, variable yields.

Real-Time Van Sales Integration

Replace batch sync with real-time connection. Create routes as SAP locations for P&L visibility.

Component-Level Costing

Implement fat/protein/lactose tracking for dairy. Joint cost allocation by market value for poultry.

Medium-term (6-12 months)
LIMS Integration

Connect laboratory systems to SAP. Automatic quality results capture and trend analysis.

Group Reporting Implementation

SAP Group Reporting for automated consolidation. Transfer pricing documentation for corporate tax.

S/4HANA Readiness

Prepare for migration. Address costing-based to account-based CO-PA transition.

Conclusion

Audit Opinion

Qualified Opinion - Significant Remediation Required

Based on our comprehensive assessment of 457 findings across four entities, we conclude that the SAP implementation provides a foundational capability that is materially underutilized.

There are critical gaps in food safety controls (products released before micro results, traceability below regulatory standards) and material financial reporting risks (biological asset valuation, component costing, manual consolidation) that require immediate management attention.

With the recommended remediation roadmap, the Group can achieve a target maturity level of 3.5+ within 12 months, unlocking significant operational efficiency and compliance improvements.
