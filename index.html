Root Cause Analysis

Why Remediation Won't Work

The system is built on fundamentally wrong foundations

1

Wrong Module Architecture

The entire production backbone uses PP (discrete manufacturing) instead of PP-PI (process industries). This is like building a house on wrong foundation - you cannot fix it with renovations.

Requires complete production module replacement
2

Quality Management Never Implemented

SAP QM was not part of original scope. Retrofitting QM into existing production orders, batch management, and workflows would require touching every process.

Cannot bolt-on food safety to broken core
3

Fragmented System Landscape

Critical operations run in 6+ disconnected systems (Ransale, Easy Feed, LIMS, Excel). The integration debt is so severe that connecting them would cost more than replacing.

Integration complexity exceeds rebuild cost
4

Data Model Fundamentally Flawed

No batch genealogy, no catch-weight, no component tracking, no biological asset valuation. The master data structure cannot support required functionality.

Master data redesign = system rebuild
5

ECC End of Life

SAP ECC mainstream maintenance ends 2027. Any significant investment in current system is throwing money at a platform with no future.

Investment in dying platform is waste

Regulatory Risk

Food Safety & Compliance

Critical gaps that expose the group to regulatory action and recall risk

Products Released Before Test Results

Microbiological results (5 days for yeast/mold) come after product is already in market. Management acknowledged: "We sleep with that risk."

Consumer safety risk | Potential recall liability | Brand damage

4-Hour Recall Capability Not Possible

Cannot trace finished goods back to raw milk supplier or flock within 4 hours. Batch-to-batch linkage breaks at production order level.

Regulatory non-compliance | Expanded recall scope | Compliance failure

Process Parameters Not Captured

Critical parameters (pasteurization 72C/15sec, fermentation temp/time, cooking temps) NOT captured in SAP. Only exist on paper.

Cannot prove pasteurization | HACCP documentation gap

Vaccination & Medication Not Tracked

Flock health treatments recorded on paper. Cannot demonstrate compliance with withdrawal periods before slaughter.

Antibiotic residue risk | Export certification issues

Path Forward

A greenfield ERP implementation is required - the current system cannot be remediated

Phase 1: Assessment & Selection
Requirements Definition

Document detailed functional requirements for food safety, traceability, process manufacturing, and financial reporting.

Solution Evaluation

Evaluate ERP options with proven Food & Beverage industry capabilities. Consider cloud vs on-premise deployment.

Implementation Partner Selection

Select partner with demonstrated experience in dairy, poultry, and feed manufacturing implementations.

Phase 2: Core Implementation
Process Industry Production

Implement correct module for food manufacturing with recipe management, process parameters, and variable yields.

Quality Management & Traceability

Deploy integrated QM with LIMS connectivity, batch genealogy, and system-enforced quality holds.

System Integration

Connect weighbridge, laboratory systems, van sales, and production equipment in real-time.

Phase 3: Optimization
Advanced Planning

Implement demand planning with seasonal pattern recognition and shelf-life optimization.

Financial Excellence

Component-level costing, IAS 41 compliance, group consolidation, and transfer pricing.

Continuous Improvement

Analytics-driven optimization, predictive quality, and automated compliance reporting.

Final Assessment

Conclusion & Recommendation

Adverse Opinion - System Rebuild Required

After comprehensive assessment of 0 control points across four entities, examining 89 process steps and interviewing key stakeholders, we conclude that the current SAP ECC implementation is beyond remediation.

The system was built on fundamentally wrong architecture - using discrete manufacturing for process industries, omitting quality management entirely, and creating a fragmented landscape where critical operations run outside SAP. The cost and complexity of fixing these structural deficiencies would exceed a complete rebuild.

Our recommendation: Proceed with a total greenfield implementation of SAP S/4HANA with industry-specific Food & Beverage capabilities, proper PP-PI architecture, embedded quality management, and unified integration platform. This is not optional - it is the only viable path to achieve regulatory compliance, operational efficiency, and financial accuracy.
